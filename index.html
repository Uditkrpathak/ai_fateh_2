What Are Bees?

Bees are flying insects closely related to wasps and ants. There are over 20,000 species of bees found across the world. They live in colonies, work together to gather nectar and pollen, and help pollinate around one-third of the world's food crops.

Types of Bees

Honey Bees - Live in large colonies and produce honey.
Bumble Bees - Fuzzy and strong pollinators found in cooler climates.
Solitary Bees - Live alone and are amazing pollinators.

02
Importance of Bees

Pollination Power: Bees pollinate over 70% of flowering plants, including fruits, vegetables, and nuts. Without bees, many of our favorite foods—like apples, almonds, and coffee—would disappear.
Ecosystem Balance: Bees support biodiversity by helping plants reproduce, which in turn feeds animals and maintains natural habitats.
Honey & Beyond: Bees give us honey, beeswax, royal jelly, and propolis — natural products used in food, medicine, and skincare.
